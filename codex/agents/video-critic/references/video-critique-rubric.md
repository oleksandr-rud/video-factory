# Video Critique Rubric

Use this rubric to make final video critique concrete, not aesthetic hand-waving. Producer criteria and user acceptance rules are binding. Use this rubric to fill gaps and calibrate severity; do not override explicit production instructions.

## Viewer Outcome

- Hook: first seconds create a clear reason to keep watching.
- Clarity: the viewer can explain the main point after one watch.
- Payoff: the ending resolves the promise made by the hook.
- Novelty: the video adds a fresh angle rather than repeating a channel template.

## Story And Source Fit

- Scenario scenes appear in order unless an intentional edit improves the result.
- Claims match supplied source evidence, `claim_ledger[]`, source reports, or are clearly framed as interpretation.
- On-screen text and narration do not contradict each other.
- The video avoids unsupported hype, misleading simplification, and stale repetition.

## Visual Review

- Every scene has relevant visual evidence or a deliberate abstract/motion-graphics choice.
- Visuals are readable at the target aspect ratio and resolution.
- Crops, framing, and safe areas protect faces, product/UI details, subtitles, logos, and CTAs.
- AI-generated or stock clips do not introduce artifacts, watermarks, mismatched brands, or incoherent motion.
- Transitions support pacing instead of hiding weak continuity.

## Audio And Captions

- Voiceover is intelligible, paced for the platform, and free of major pronunciation problems.
- Music and SFX support motion without masking the voice.
- Captions match spoken words, stay in safe areas, and remain readable on mobile.
- Caption timing does not drift from audio or cover critical visual details.

## Platform And Delivery

- Duration, aspect ratio, fps, resolution, and codec match the requested platform.
- The opening frame, thumbnail candidate, title/text hooks, and CTA fit the channel format.
- Rights notes cover stock, AI generation, voices, music, likeness, logos, source screenshots, approved web images, and copied page/article material.

## Finding Severity

- Blocker: should not deliver until fixed.
- Major: materially hurts comprehension, trust, platform fit, or brand quality.
- Minor: noticeable polish issue that does not block delivery.
- Note: useful preference, experiment, or future improvement.
